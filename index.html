Why it exists

Your build went red. You're at dinner.

On-call at 2am. Laptop in the overhead bin. A hotfix that can't wait. None of it needs your desk — just git, safely, from your pocket.

The rules it won't break

An app that pushes git from your phone should scare you.

So it can't do the dangerous things. Safety is what the app is allowed to do — not a setting you remember to flip.

  • Pull is fast-forward only. Diverged? It stops and tells you — never a blind merge.
  • Push never forces. There's no force flag anywhere in the app.
  • Every action is identity-attributed. Never the wrong email at 11pm.
  • Discard shows the diff first. Nothing vanishes silently.

Anti-features, on purpose

What it won't do.

  • ✕ No force push
  • ✕ No hard reset
  • ✕ No rebase from your phone
  • ✕ No non-fast-forward merge
  • ✕ No silent identity mix-ups
  • ✕ No keys leaving your daemon

The laptop is still where you do anything you can't undo.

No lock-in

It's still just git.

A window into repos already on disk, via simple-git. No new remote, no proprietary history. Uninstall the daemon and nothing changed.

One binary

How it works.

One bun --compile binary. Point it at your folders; it serves the dashboard over a Cloudflare tunnel. Sign in with Connections (OIDC / PKCE).

bash
$ repoyeti add-root ~/code # scan for repos $ repoyeti start # daemon up, get your phone URL
