The moment it exists for

Your build just went red. You're at dinner.

On-call fires at 2am. A client demo starts in ten minutes and staging is still on yesterday's branch. Your laptop's in the overhead bin. You merged something you shouldn't have and need to revert it before a teammate pulls.

None of these need you at your desk — they need git, safely, from whatever's in your hand. That's the whole premise.

The rules it won't break

An app that pushes git from your phone should scare you.

So RepoYeti is built so it can't do the dangerous things. The safety isn't a setting you remember to turn on — it's what the app is allowed to do at all.

  • Pull is fast-forward only. If your branch has diverged, it stops and tells you — it never merges something you didn't ask for.
  • Push never forces. There is no force flag anywhere in the app. Full stop.
  • Every action is identity-attributed. Commit, pull and push run as the git identity you picked for that repo — no pushing a work commit under your personal email at 11pm.
  • Discarding a file shows the diff first. Nothing disappears silently.

Every repo, one screen

The whole machine at a glance — not one repo at a time.

RepoYeti finds every git repo on your machine and tracks each one — branch, dirty, ahead, behind — in real time. Not on a polling interval: the daemon watches the filesystem and your remotes, so state changes the second they happen.

↻ Fetch all, in one tap

Refresh every remote across every repo at once — so you know which of your forty repos drifted overnight before you even open your laptop. Green means clean; a number means something moved.

◈ Live, event-driven

Commit from a terminal in another window, a CI bot merges a PR, someone force-pushes upstream — the dashboard updates the moment it happens. No spinner, no 30-second refresh loop.

⚑ Pin, star, hide

Filter by folder or identity, pin the repos you babysit, star your daily drivers, and hide the archived fork you cloned in 2019. Nothing to register per-repo — it's discovered.

⌥ Many identities, by design

Work email on work repos, personal on side projects, a client's identity on their contract work. RepoYeti knows which is which per-repo — no --amend --author panic three commits deep.

Smart Commit — AI, bring your own key

It reads your mess. You approve the plan.

You've been heads-down for three hours and your working tree is fourteen files across two features and a typo fix. Smart Commit reads the diff, groups the changes by intent, and hands you a plan: commit 1 is the auth fix, commit 2 is the new endpoint, commit 3 is the typo. Edit it inline — move files between commits, reorder, rename — or tap once and let it run.

Bring your own key: Groq, OpenRouter, Gemini, Claude, ChatGPT or DeepSeek. Your key stays on your daemon. Your code only ever leaves your machine to reach the model you picked.

Anti-features, on purpose

What it won't do.

Naming the dangerous things it refuses to ship is the point — this is a phone, not a place for surgery.

  • ✕ No force push
  • ✕ No hard reset
  • ✕ No rebase from your phone
  • ✕ No merge unless it fast-forwards
  • ✕ No silent identity mix-ups
  • ✕ No API keys leaving your daemon

The phone keeps things moving; the laptop is still where you do anything you can't undo.

No lock-in

It's still just git.

RepoYeti is a window into repos that already exist on disk, using simple-git under the hood. No new remote, no proprietary history, no walled garden. Uninstall the daemon and your repos are exactly as you left them.

One binary, your machine, your rules

How it works.

A single bun --compile binary. The CLI is the launcher — point it at the folders you want scanned and it finds every repo, then serves the dashboard over a Cloudflare tunnel so your phone reaches your own machine directly. Sign in with Connections (OIDC / PKCE — no password to invent or leak).

bash
$ repoyeti add-root ~/code   # scan a folder for repos $ repoyeti start           # boot the daemon, get your phone URL

Built on Bun · bun:sqlite · Hono · simple-git · a Vue 3 + Tailwind PWA
